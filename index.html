Computing and IT project

Royal Mail vehicle and personal digital assistant (PDA) system

This was the final piece of work for The Open University. Coming up with our own ideas for a project piece and planning every stage from start to finish, using previous modules and incorporating them where possible. My project was based upon the modules Fundamentals of interaction design, data management and analysis and web, mobile and cloud technologies.

My project was based around a problem I face every day at Royal Mail when signing a vehicle and PDA out. This problem was the form being incorrectly completed and not being completed in full. To overcome this problem and to ensure the integrity of the data that was recorded, the creation of a hybrid mobile app to guide users through the procedure and place constraints on the inputs to help alleviate these issues.

The major tasks identified to being key to a successful project were:

  • Initial interviews with all stakeholders to understand the most important requirements
  • Paper prototyping
  • Evaluation of paper prototype
  • Reviewing of evaluation and the completion of a higher fidelity prototype
  • Evaluate higher fidelity prototype
  • Database design and building
  • Scenarios and test cases
  • Building of hybrid mobile app

The initial interviews were conducted with a staff member from each of the stakeholder groups and done in an office at the depot. Researching materials for carrying out successful interviews was key to this procedure and I discovered some important questions to ask. The most useful being "What is the #1 biggest problem you face currently and how is this resolved?". This question lead to the largest issue being members of staff filling out the wrong row on the current paper based form.

The complete list of functional requirements of the app were as follows:

  • Driver sign out
  • Driver sign in
  • PDA sign out
  • PDA sign in
  • Add a new van
  • Remove an existing van
  • Search the vehicle log

With all requirements identified, a paper prototype was produced. Paper prototyping allows for a quick and cheap design to be tested. This is important at an early stage to ensure that designers are more open to changes proposed by the participants. It also allows for designers to draw directly onto the prototype making changes are required.

When conducting evaluations on the paper prototype, I was looking for feedback on the structure of the pages and how successfully users could navigate through each process. Some of the suggested changes were moving elements around to group them into a more logical order, for example, moving "Reason for adding" above "Added by" on the add a new van page. Management also requested that some of the processes be kept away from some users, such as the Adding a van, Removing a van and Viewing the log.

The successful completion of the paper prototype took me to evaluating the results and then producing a higher fidelity version, which would provide users some interaction with the prototype and allow me to test how long some of the processes took. The process I was most eager to assess was the driver signing out process, as this was the longest of all the processes, involving 3 separate stages and a lot of information for users to enter. Due to the busy nature of the office with all staff leaving around the same time each day, if the process was time consuming and difficult to do, users would oppose its use. The higher fidelity prototype was produced using Pencil.
